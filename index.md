# Shibani Singh

I’m interested in building AI systems that are not only powerful, but efficient, trustworthy, and useful in the real world.

My work sits at the intersection of AI systems, machine learning, systems engineering, and scientific computing. I like problems where software, algorithms, and hardware all meet, especially when small improvements in efficiency or reliability can make AI more practical for researchers, clinicians, and engineers.

A lot of what motivates me comes down to this: AI should help us do better science, solve harder problems, and use our resources more responsibly (hopefully lesser carbon footprint from advancements in the future?). I’m interested in systems that make AI faster, safer, more energy-efficient, and easier to deploy in meaningful settings.

---

## Research Interests

### AI Systems & Infrastructure

I’m interested in the systems behind modern AI, including optimizations, GPU performance, distributed training, inference optimization, and scalable machine learning infrastructure.

### Efficient Machine Learning

I care about making AI more efficient, not just more capable. This includes model optimization, profiling, hardware-aware algorithms, and ways to reduce compute cost and energy use without losing performance.

### Trustworthy AI

I work on methods that make machine learning systems more reliable, secure, and privacy-aware. This includes privacy-preserving learning, secure computation, robustness, and responsible deployment.

### AI for Science & Healthcare

My early work in applied machine learning focused on medical imaging and healthcare. I try to keep abreast with AI developed to support scientific discovery, biomedical research, and other areas where better tools can have a real human impact.

---

## What Motivates Me

The problems I find most interesting usually do not fit neatly into one field.

I like combining systems engineering, machine learning, and scientific research to build tools that help people understand and improve complex AI workloads.

Long term, I want to work on AI infrastructure that helps researchers do more with fewer resources — whether that means reducing energy use, lowering compute costs, or making powerful tools more accessible to people working on important problems.

## Featured Projects

* **AI Systems Performance**
  Performance profiling, GPU analysis, optimizations, and inference optimization for modern AI workloads.

* **Privacy-Preserving Machine Learning**
  Differential privacy, and secure approaches for learning from sensitive or distributed data.

* **Scientific Machine Learning**
  Applying machine learning to healthcare, biomedical research, and scientific computing.

* **Systems Programming**
  Rust and C++ projects focused on performance, tooling, and low-level systems.

## Current Questions

* How can we make AI systems much more compute and energy-efficient without giving up capability?
* How can systems software make better use of modern accelerators?
* What does it take to build AI that is powerful, trustworthy, and practical to deploy?
* How can AI infrastructure help accelerate scientific discovery?
* What tools do researchers and engineers need to understand and optimize complex AI workloads?
